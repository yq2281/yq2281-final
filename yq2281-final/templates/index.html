(Side note: Isn't it crazy how modern photography has completely eradicated the novelty of owning a likeness of yourself? Before, you had to have a drawing or painting of yourself to truly be in possession of a tangible or immutable representation of your face! I often think about the subjectivities of human vision and the dangers that this proliferation of distorted representations of personhood (i.e., photographs) can inflict on our self-images. When we view other forms of art, we understand it as being two steps removed from reality, abstracted by first the symbols (e.g., the shapes, textures, planes, etc.) which an artist utilizes to convey a subject matter, and then subsequently our visual-to-mental-to-visual translation of these symbols into our own perception of said subject matter. But there's this notion for some reason that technology is inherently objective and that somehow photographs represent a dry visual truth which cannot be conveyed through other ways of seeing. Crazy how the photograph dominates our visual consciousness! I think human visual perception, and especially our perception of other humans, is so much more complex and intricate than that! So that's why this is one of my favorite photographs of myself... Not only because I look GREAT, but because it truly captures the happiness and confidence I felt in the moment, surrounded by wonderful friends and feeling loved!)
